Detection & Alignment
· The Most Important Stage ·
—MediaPipe BlazeFace: Google's mobile-optimized detector. Returns bounding box + 6 keypoints (eyes, nose, mouth, ears).
—Why not Haar? No keypoints → can't align. Fails on tilted faces.
—Affine Transform: uses right eye, left eye, nose tip to compute warp matrix M.
$$[x_{out}, y_{out}]^T = M \cdot [x_{in}, y_{in}, 1]^T$$
Fixed output positions in 100×100: Right eye → (35,40) · Left eye → (65,40) · Nose → (50,65)
Result: regardless of head tilt, scale, or distance — the face always looks the same to PCA and LDA. This is why the pipeline works.

PCA — Overview
—Problem: 10,000-D space with 325 training images = Curse of Dimensionality. Model memorises instead of learning.
—Solution: PCA finds the 50 directions of greatest variance → compresses 10,000 → 50 keeping 93.6% of information.
· Steps ·
1Grayscale: \(\text{Gray} = 0.587G + 0.299R + 0.114B\)
2Global Mean: \(\mu = \frac{1}{M}\sum x_i\) — the "average face"
3Mean-Centering: \(\Phi_i = x_i - \mu\) → Matrix A (point cloud)
4Covariance: \(C = \frac{1}{M}A \cdot A^T\) — "do pixel i and j move together?"
5Eigenvectors: \(C \cdot u = \lambda \cdot u\) → 10,000 directions, keep best 50
6Projection: \(w = U_k^T \cdot (x - \mu)\) → fingerprint = 50 numbers
💡 Each eigenvector drawn as 100×100 image = Eigenface. Each w_k = "how much of Eigenface k is in this face?"

LDA — Overview
—Problem with PCA alone: PCA captures largest variance = often lighting, not identity. Same person in bright vs dark → PCA sees two strangers.
—LDA is supervised — it knows identities from dataset labels. Input: 50 numbers from PCA. Output: 5 numbers.

Knows identities?✗ Unsupervised✓ Supervised

Output dims505 (= n_classes − 1)
LDA goal: same-person images cluster together AND different-person images are far apart in the projected space.

LDA — Equations
· Class Mean ·
$$\mu_c = \frac{1}{n_c}\sum_{x \in X_c} x$$
· Within-Class Scatter ·
$$S^W = \sum_c\sum_x(x-\mu_c)(x-\mu_c)^T$$
Want Sᵂ SMALL — same person clustered tightly
· Between-Class Scatter ·
$$S_B = \sum_c n_c(\mu_c-\mu)(\mu_c-\mu)^T$$
Want Sʙ LARGE — people far from each other
· Fisher Criterion + Solution ·
$$J(w)=\frac{w^TS_Bw}{w^TS^Ww}$$
$$(S^W)^{-1}S_B \cdot w = \lambda w$$
💡 Why PCA before LDA? Direct LDA on 10k dims → Sᵂ is 10k×10k singular matrix (not invertible). PCA compresses to 50 first → solvable.

SVM Classification
—Input: 5 numbers from LDA. Draws decision boundaries with Maximum Margin between classes.
· Margin Objective ·
$$\min \frac{1}{2}\|w\|^2 + C\sum\xi_i$$
C = 10penalty — chosen by grid search
Support Vectorsclosest points to boundary
· RBF Kernel ·
$$K(x,y)=\exp(-\gamma\|x-y\|^2)$$
· Platt Scaling ·
$$P=\frac{1}{1+\exp(A\cdot f(z)+B)}$$
$$\hat{c} = \begin{cases}\text{name} & P \geq 0.40 \\ \text{Unknown} & P < 0.40\end{cases}$$
—RBF handles non-linear boundaries. Platt converts raw SVM score → real probability needed for Unknown rejection.

Two Types of Features
· Geometric Features ·
—MediaPipe's 6 keypoints: eye, eye, nose, mouth, ears.
—Used only for alignment — discarded after the affine warp.
NOT used for identification — only 6 keypoints, too coarse.
· Appearance Features ·
—PCA projects onto 50 Eigenfaces → 50 projection coefficients.
—LDA further compresses to 5 discriminative dimensions.
These ARE used for identification — w vector = face fingerprint.
Defense answer: Two types. Geometric features (keypoints) are used only for alignment, then discarded. Appearance features (PCA→LDA projection) are used for identification.

LDA Clustering
—Does the model cluster? Implicitly yes — LDA produces a space where same-person faces naturally cluster. SVM learns boundaries between those clusters.
· From the LDA Scatter Plot ·
✓abod — tight isolated cluster (top right) → perfect precision and recall.
✓aziz — isolated cluster (bottom right) → perfect precision and recall.
✓Unknown — large but well-separated from all named classes.
⚠Abdu2, Ahmad, Aseel — overlapping central region → source of the single error.
Defense answer: Clustering happens implicitly as a side effect of LDA's optimization. LDA maximises between-class scatter over within-class scatter — mathematically equivalent to a soft clustering objective. The SVM classifies in the resulting clustered space.

Limitations & Future Work
· Limitations ·
⚠Lighting: significant change → model outputs Unknown (documented in real test).
⚠Pose: yaw/pitch >30° → MediaPipe keypoints fail.
⚠Occlusion: mask, glasses, hair covering keypoints.
⚠Closed-world bias: similar-looking stranger may be misclassified as a registered person.
· Future Work v2 ·
→Keep MediaPipe + Affine Alignment.
→Replace PCA+LDA with FaceNet / ArcFace — 128/512-D deep embedding.
→Lighting robustness, one-shot enrollment, better Ahmad↔Abdu2 separation.
Deep embeddings learn structural features, not pixel intensity → immune to the single error we observed.
